# QC v1 for Rendered Video (P9-S5)

## Overview
This artifact outlines the quality control (QC) process for rendered videos in OnionReel, ensuring that videos meet specified criteria for duration, resolution, black frames, and basic loudness. The process will also include the generation of export packs in three different durations: 30 seconds, 15 seconds, and 6 seconds.

## Inputs
- Rendered video files
- QC checklist (duration, resolution, black frames, loudness)
- Audio analysis tool
- Video analysis tool
- Export settings for 30s, 15s, and 6s packs

## Outputs
- QC report detailing findings
- Exported video packs (30s, 15s, 6s)
- Log of any issues found and corrective actions taken

## Steps
1. **Initial Setup**
   - Gather all rendered video files.
   - Ensure all necessary tools (audio/video analysis) are installed and configured.

2. **Duration Check**
   - Verify that the video duration matches the expected length.
   - Document any discrepancies.

3. **Resolution Verification**
   - Check that the video resolution meets the specified standards.
   - Record any deviations.

4. **Black Frame Detection**
   - Use video analysis tools to scan for black frames.
   - Note the occurrence and duration of any detected black frames.

5. **Loudness Measurement**
   - Analyze audio levels using the audio analysis tool.
   - Ensure loudness is within acceptable limits and document findings.

6. **Generate QC Report**
   - Compile all findings into a comprehensive QC report.
   - Highlight any issues and recommended actions.

7. **Export Video Packs**
   - Create export packs for 30s, 15s, and 6s using the specified settings.
   - Ensure each pack is correctly formatted and meets quality standards.

8. **Final Review**
   - Conduct a final review of the QC report and exported packs.
   - Make any necessary adjustments before finalizing.

## Pitfalls
- **Inconsistent Tools**: Ensure all tools used for analysis are calibrated and consistent to avoid false positives/negatives.
- **Overlooking Minor Issues**: Small discrepancies in duration or resolution can lead to larger problems; be thorough in checks.
- **Export Settings Errors**: Double-check export settings to ensure that video packs are generated correctly.
- **Documentation Gaps**: Maintain detailed records of findings to aid in future QC processes and troubleshooting.
